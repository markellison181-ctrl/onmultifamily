## The Renovation Paradox

I presented a valuation to an owner two years ago. Solid building, roughly 40% of units at market. He went all-in on turns. Kitchens, bathrooms, flooring. Two years later, he's turned a meaningful percentage of the building.

We spoke last week. The value? **Roughly the same.**

He pushed NOI higher. But the market moved the other direction. Rates rose, cap rates expanded. The gains cancelled out.

This surprises people, but it shouldn't. **Building value is a function of NOI and cap rates.** You control one. You don't control the other. The owner who says "if I just turn 15 more units, the value goes up by $1M" is doing math in a vacuum. That only works if cap rates stay constant. They don't.

It goes further. Cap rates compress for value-add and expand for stabilized. A buyer pays a premium for upside. A fully renovated building with no remaining upside trades at a higher cap rate. Past roughly 80% at market, the incremental return on each turn diminishes, and can go negative after reno costs, vacancy, and the cap rate shift.

**The point isn't don't renovate. It's know when to sell.** Renovations and market timing work together. The owners who've done best improved their buildings *and* went to market when conditions aligned. That's the conversation worth having.

## Free Boiler? Close to It.

A client brought this up recently. I dug in. It's real and underutilized.

**The City of Toronto's Energy Retrofit Loan program** offers fixed-rate financing for up to **100% of project costs**, terms up to **30 years**, pegged to the City's cost of borrowing. First year is **interest-free and payment-free.** Covers boilers, heat pumps, building automation, insulation, lighting.

Toronto's **Hi-RIS program** works similarly. Repaid through your property tax bill, attached to the property. Transfers on sale.

**The trade-off:** you generally can't file an AGI for work financed through these programs. But if you're on MLI Select, the energy efficiency improvements may improve your scoring on refi.

Beyond Toronto:

- **Enbridge** offers the Commercial Custom Retrofit Program for gas-heated buildings (deadline **June 30, 2026**)
- Several Ontario municipalities offer **PACE-style financing**
- Worth a call to your local government. The landscape is evolving fast.

## Open vs. Closed Mortgage

This comes up at the worst time. When we're trying to close a sale.

- Some owners take a closed mortgage to save a few bps on their rate
- On exit, that means: an **IRD penalty** well into six figures, a **mortgage assumption** that locks the buyer into your terms, or **portability** that rarely lines up
- Every buyer has a different strategy. MLI Select, conventional, bridge, lower leverage.
- Forcing an assumption **narrows your buyer pool and costs you on price**
- Get the open structure. The spread difference is minimal. The flexibility on exit is not.

Same logic for locking in a new mortgage pre-sale thinking the buyer will assume it. Don't. Go to market with clean title or easily dischargeable debt. Let the buyer arrange their own financing.

## Prep Before You Sell

**Dragging out a sale does not improve pricing.** Our typical process:

| Phase | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Listing agreement to market launch | 3–4 weeks |
| On market to offers finalized | 3–4 weeks |
| Conditional period | 0–45 days |
| Conditions waived to closing | 30–60 days |
| **Total** | **~3 to 5 months** |

📸 *[GRAPHIC: Simple horizontal timeline showing the 4 phases]*

The variance is almost entirely how prepared the seller is. Three things worth doing now if you're considering a sale in the next 12–24 months:

- **Phase I ESA** from a reputable firm. Eliminates the #1 source of deal delays, re-trades, and failed transactions. Budget **$5K–$10K.**
- **Cosmetic touch-ups.** Paint in common areas, clean hallways, working lights. First impressions flow directly into pricing.
- **LED lighting.** Lobbies, stairwells, parking, exteriors. Cheap, immediately visible.

All three: **under $20K.** Worth every dollar.
